ACERCA DE

ABOUT

The designation of Quito as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1978 (along with that of Krakow, Poland) broke new ground in that it represented the first time that a city was inscribed on the World Heritage list. The visionary Quito inscription recognized both Quito’s remarkable artistic traditions and also its innovative settlement patterns that for centuries have brought nature and people together in a complex topographical environment, making them a proven template for urban resilience. The Quito listing signaled to the world that cultural heritage was not limited to iconic monuments but unfolds at a landscape scale embracing the whole of living cities. In so doing, it helped change forever the way we think about urban heritage.

In October 2016, almost 40 years later, the world’s attention is once again on Quito as thousands of delegates and observers assemble there for the Third United Nations Conference on Housing and Sustainable Urban Development (Habitat 3). And again, Quito’s patrimony has been mobilized, this time to show the world the critical role that contemporary notions of cultural heritage play in the achievement of the new, more humanistic and ecological paradigm of a sustainable city embodied in the New Urban Agenda – Habitat 3’s ambition outcome document.

Vive Alameda – a Habitat 3 Urban Village project that invites you to literally and digitally stroll the byways of Quito’s historic Barrio La Alameda neighborhood during the week of October 17. Using the accompanying map of specially designed places and schedule of events, visitors alike will be treated to an authentic introduction to the neighborhood’s s rich mix of historic buildings, green, public spaces and cultural traditions. Onsite and online interpretive materials have been specially designed to facilitate a rich dialogue about how these heritage elements can contribute to a sustainable vision of the future. The Vive Alameda Habitat 3 Village project will culminate in a live ‘charrette’ where international visitors, local experts and neighborhood stakeholders will work together to answer these questions.

The Vive Alameda project was developed through an innovative collaboration between the IMP and LINQ, community stakeholders and international heritage experts assembled by the International Council on Monuments and Sites (ICOMOS) working together in a participatory process. It was designed to both leave behind a useful tool for the neighborhood and to provide Habitat 3 delegates and observers with a living demonstration of how their own cities can achieve UN Sustainable Development Goal 11.4 -- which calls for making cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable by strengthening efforts to protect and safeguard the world’s cultural and natural heritage.

The Habitat 3 New Urban Agenda and the UN Sustainable Development Goals recognize that we now live in what has been termed the urban century, one in which the sustainability and resilience of cities and towns will be key. These documents also break new ground in recognizing that cultural heritage and urban sustainability are inseparable. Whether your community is far along the path to this realization or just beginning, Vive Alameda promises to be a crossroads on this urban journey. Join us there!
